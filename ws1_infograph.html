Supervised Learning

01 / 10

Linear Regression

LR — Statistical Workhorse
Supervised Tabular

Fits a straight line through data points by minimizing squared error. Predicts a continuous numeric output as a weighted sum of input features.

Real-world uses

House price prediction · sales forecasting · estimating insurance premiums

02 / 10

Logistic Regression

LogReg — Binary Classifier
Supervised Tabular

Wraps a linear model in a sigmoid function to output probabilities between 0 and 1. Despite its name, it solves classification — not regression.

Real-world uses

Spam detection · disease diagnosis · loan default prediction

03 / 10

Decision Trees

DT — Rule-Based Splits
Supervised Tabular

Recursively splits the data on the feature that best separates outcomes, building a tree of if-then rules. Highly interpretable but prone to overfitting.

Real-world uses

Credit scoring · customer churn analysis · medical triage

04 / 10

Random Forest

RF — Ensemble of Trees
Supervised Tabular

Trains hundreds of decision trees on random subsets of data and features, then averages their votes. Trades some interpretability for much stronger accuracy.

Real-world uses

Fraud detection · feature-importance ranking · stock movement prediction

05 / 10

Support Vector Machines

SVM — Margin Maximizer
Supervised Tabular Computer Vision

Finds the hyperplane that separates classes with the widest possible margin. Kernel tricks let it bend into curved decision boundaries in high-dimensional space.

Real-world uses

Text categorization · handwritten digit recognition · gene-expression classification

06 / 10

Convolutional Neural Networks

CNN — Spatial Pattern Detector
Supervised Computer Vision

Stacks learnable filters that slide across images to detect edges, textures, shapes, and objects. Deeper layers compose simple features into complex concepts.

Real-world uses

Medical imaging diagnosis · self-driving car perception · facial recognition

07 / 10

Transformers

TFM — Attention Is All You Need
Supervised NLP Generative AI

Uses self-attention to weigh how every token in a sequence relates to every other. Trained on massive text corpora, the architecture powers modern large language models.

Real-world uses

ChatGPT & Claude · machine translation · code generation · document summarization

§ 02

Unsupervised Learning

Finds structure without labels
08 / 10

K-Means Clustering

K-Means — Centroid Grouping
Unsupervised Tabular

Picks k cluster centers and iteratively reassigns each point to its nearest center, then recomputes the centers, until groups stabilize.

Real-world uses

Customer segmentation · image color quantization · anomaly detection in logs

09 / 10

Principal Component Analysis

PCA — Dimensionality Reducer
Unsupervised Tabular

Identifies the directions (principal components) along which data varies most, projecting high-dimensional data into fewer dimensions while preserving the signal.

Real-world uses

Genomics visualization · noise reduction in sensor data · feature compression before modeling

10 / 10

Generative Adversarial Networks

GAN — Generator vs. Discriminator
Unsupervised Computer Vision Generative AI

Two neural networks compete: a generator invents fake samples while a discriminator tries to spot them. Both improve until the fakes look indistinguishable from real data.

Real-world uses

Photorealistic image synthesis · deepfakes · super-resolution · synthetic training data

The Lineage

How these ten algorithms relate — a progression from interpretable statistical models, through ensemble methods, to the deep architectures driving today's generative AI.

Era 01 · pre-2000s

Statistical & Geometric Foundations

The classical toolkit. Models are mathematically transparent, computationally cheap, and still dominate tabular problems in industry today.

Era 02 · 2000s–2010s

Ensembles & Deep Learning's Rise

Combining many weak learners beat single complex ones. Meanwhile, GPUs made deep neural networks practical — CNNs cracked computer vision in 2012.

  • Random Forest
  • Convolutional Neural Networks
Era 03 · 2014–present

The Generative Revolution

Networks stopped just classifying and started creating. Attention replaced recurrence, and scale produced emergent capabilities no one predicted.

  • Generative Adversarial Networks (2014)
